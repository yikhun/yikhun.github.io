2026 World Cup · 1B Target Review (Final ver.)

Results and next steps, for the marketing team

Data window: Jun 12 – Jul 20, 2026 (GMT+8, 39 days, tournament complete) Scope: OT (12bet) platform Sources: Admin backend (core totals) + Looker (dimension detail, full 39-day export)

The conclusions first

  1. "1B" is confirmed as absolute platform volume during the tournament window. Final achievement: 35.1%. The 39-day Admin-basis Valid TO closed at $351.0M ($361.9M before removing void bets, 36.2%). How big was the gap? Hitting 1B needed $25.6M a day; the actual daily average was $9.0M, meaning the platform would have had to produce about 111 days of normal volume in 39 days. No amount of marketing effort covers that multiple. The event-isolation section below puts real numbers on it: the target asked for +194% over baseline, and the World Cup actually delivered +3.1%, a gap of roughly 60x. The 35.1% says more about the target's scale than about anyone's execution.
  2. World Cup campaigns are nearly invisible in the tracking system. Query by the Event tag or the Source tag; either way, turnover explicitly marked as World Cup sits at 0.03%–0.05%, and 99.80% of volume rides on the pre-existing "Old Event" mechanism. For a tournament the whole platform was pushing, attribution that low points first to tags failing to catch the traffic, not to players ignoring the campaigns. Until the tracking question is settled, every campaign-effectiveness number in this report reads as a floor, not a verdict.
  3. Within what the attribution system can see, MKT-controlled channels contributed about 2.3%. Paid Ads, SEO, Social Media, KOL, and the currency CM teams plus smaller lines add up to roughly $8.13M, or 2.26% of platform Valid TO; Organic (58.56%) and the Affiliate team (39.18%) carry over 97.7%. This 2.26% comes from the same attribution system as the point above, so if tag coverage is broken, it is understated too.
  4. Twelve provider/game-type lines finished with negative GGR, about $168,680 in losses (1.39% of total GGR). Pronet (PG Sports) and TCG SEA Lottery account for 78% of it. Small against the total, but worth a look from Risk (details in the risk section).

Three pieces of information we'd like MKT to share

This report only has platform-side data, so many of the "why" questions stop at hypotheses. That isn't fair to MKT: contribution the tracking system can't see will never be reconstructed from platform data alone. If MKT can share the three items below with the AT team, the two halves of the picture come together, the campaign's real performance becomes measurable, and whatever the attribution system has been undercounting finally surfaces:

The baseline is the 39 days immediately before kickoff (May 4 – Jun 11, 2026): same length, same basis, compared directly. This section answers the report's central question: how much did the World Cup actually add?

Finding one: the World Cup lifted Sportsbook 14.8%, but total platform volume rose only 3.1%, because Non-sports fell 7.9% over the same stretch. Sportsbook's share climbed from 48.7% to 54.2%. Part of the tournament's volume moved over from other product lines rather than arriving as new money. Whether that's players shifting budget out of Casino and Lottery to bet on the World Cup, or Non-sports simply drifting down on its own, aggregate data can't distinguish. It takes player-level cross-analysis (did the same people cut their non-sports betting) to confirm.

Finding two: TO rose 3.1%, GGR stayed flat. Margin slipped from 3.48% to 3.37% during the tournament. Directionally you could say the World Cup brought volume rather than profit, but the GGR difference (−0.02%) is far smaller than the methodology's own error margin (see the notes at the bottom), so flat is the only defensible reading. It did not earn less.

Finding three: against the baseline, the target outran the event's actual pull by roughly 60x. Hitting 1B meant producing 194% more than baseline; the World Cup actually added +3.1%. A 35.1% achievement rate is not an execution story. The target simply exceeded what this kind of event can move. Next time, these two numbers (a $340M baseline, an event lift of about 3%) are the starting point.

What this means for MKT: +3.1% platform-wide sounds small, but Sportsbook's +14.8% is a real lift, and Bet User grew 3.1% (about 800 people). The priority next time isn't "make the event pull more volume." It's two things: keep the Sportsbook gain from being eaten by a Non-sports slide (if the shift is real, cross-sell design is the lever), and make the lift attributable, so MKT's contribution is visible.

For MKT: the evidence, and what to do next time

Evidence one: WC campaign tracking is nearly empty, which is not the same as "the campaigns didn't work"

The Event and Source dimensions both measure 0.03%–0.05%, with Old Event soaking up 99.80%, across the full 39-day window.

Three explanations are possible, and they are not mutually exclusive. One: exposure never reached players' betting path, if WC entry points lived only on a promo page rather than the homepage or the bet flow. Two: tags weren't applied, so players who came for the World Cup were never recognized as such. The fact that Old Event absorbs 99.8% of volume makes this the one to rule out first. Three: exposure landed but the incentive wasn't compelling, and players stuck with the existing mechanics. Telling them apart requires pulling real betting records. The aggregate numbers cannot answer "why."

Evidence two: attribution sees a 2.26% MKT contribution, and that number is a floor for now

Paid Ads + SEO + Social + KOL + CM teams + smaller lines total about 2.26%; Organic + Affiliate team carry over 97.7%.

Platform-side data alone cannot distinguish "MKT didn't spend more" from "MKT spent, but attribution missed it." There is also a structural possibility: if MKT's campaigns did bring players in, but those players were logged as Organic or Affiliate once they landed, the attribution chain broke after the landing, and MKT's contribution is hiding inside that 58.56% Organic share where platform data will never see it. The only way to tell is to lay MKT's actual spend records (how much, where, when it went live) against this attribution output, segment by segment.

Evidence three: one day above 5.7% Margin in 39 days, and the final fell back the next day

Jul 19 (the third-place match day) was the period's Margin peak; the final on Jul 20 dropped back to 2.24%.

This is blended platform Margin, not the World Cup book's own performance. Right now the data shows what happened but not why. Players may have concentrated on low-Margin markets (moneyline, say) on final day, or other product lines may have dragged the blend down that day. Both are hypotheses, and confirming either takes daily data split by product line.

What to do next time

Check whether tracking is broken before judging whether campaigns worked. Sample players who verifiably came through a WC banner or claimed a WC bonus, and check whether their Event/Source tags landed. This belongs ahead of every other discussion. If tracking has a real gap, MKT's contribution is badly understated and the premise behind every other number collapses.

Reconcile actual spend against attribution. The paid share came in low, so lay finance's real media-spend records against Looker's attribution and rule out "the money was spent, the tracking just never saw it."

Launch earlier, and put it where people actually bet. For the next major tournament, get campaigns live at least two weeks out, with the entry point in the main bet flow or the app homepage rather than a promo-page banner. This is what the tracking numbers point to indirectly: Old Event absorbing 99.8% of volume means the World Cup campaigns never intercepted players' betting path.

Switch to an uplift target instead of event-window absolute volume. This time 1B was defined as absolute platform volume during the window, and the result was 35.1%. Absolute targets have a structural flaw: 97.7% of the volume comes from the Organic and Affiliate base that MKT cannot move, however well or badly it performs. A target framed as "X% above ordinary baseline during the event" makes the numerator something campaigns can actually influence, and achievement starts to measure MKT's real performance. The baseline is now measured: 39 days at $340M, with the World Cup adding 3.1% platform-wide and 14.8% to Sportsbook. Next time's target starts from there.

Tournament rhythm: match value climbs, match density thins out

The group stage ran 72 matches over 17 days (4.2 matches a day on average). The knockout rounds spread 32 matches across the following 22 days, with 5 days carrying no matches at all and only 10 matches in the final 13 days. Average match value climbed as the tournament went on (the four brands' knockout-round average sat at 1.6 to 2.1 times their group-stage average), but match density fell even faster, so daily betting volume actually peaked during the group stage and the Round of 32, and total volume in the back half of the knockout rounds shrank.

The common thread across this stretch is a negative Margin, meaning bettors collectively came out ahead. The actual mechanism (say, how often favorites advanced, or how odds were set) would need market-level data to confirm; what's shown here is the Margin distribution by stage, not a claim about why. Pinning down which markets the losses concentrate in (moneyline, handicap, totals) would need bet-level data, and right now the data only goes down to the match level. Profit recovery concentrated after the quarterfinals, with Margin turning positive across every stage from there. The last 8 matches from the quarterfinals on made up 7.7% of matches and 13.1% of TO, but delivered 38.1% of the tournament's GGR, a per-match profit rate 7.4x the first 96 matches.

The single biggest day was 06/30, when three Round-of-32 matches combined for roughly $2.76M across the four brands, with Netherlands vs Morocco the largest single match outside the final (by TO). The final itself brought in $1.47M and 33,888 bets, the largest single match of the tournament by TO. But if you're looking at profit rather than volume, the real winner was the semifinal between France and Spain: 12bet's Margin on that match hit 27.2%, with GGR of $187,738, the single biggest GGR of any match in the tournament. Big occasions clearly draw money. There just aren't many of them in one tournament.

12bet: highest volume, thinnest cut per dollar wagered

It alone carries 73% of the four brands' TO, but keeps just $3.14 per $100 wagered, the lowest of the four.

12bet's average bet size runs $50.4, the highest of the four. That volume-and-Margin combination fits the profile of large-stake, price-conscious bettors: the kind who look for whichever provider's odds sit closest to true probability. The closer the odds sit to true probability, the thinner the Margin left for the house. Worth flagging: this is an inference drawn from the shape of the data, not something confirmed by actual customer research, the same caveat that applies to the Nova88/Goal123 comparison below. What's confirmed is the provider-concentration numbers; the customer motivation itself remains an inference.

This tournament, Pinnacle kept just $1.64 per $100 of TO, and BTI kept $1.80, both well below 12bet's overall $3.14. Neither number points to a trading mistake. That's simply how this customer base bets.

For 12bet, pushing more volume through the same base has limited upside; the base is already large. The better lever is getting the same customers to place more bets on higher-Margin formats, like parlays. Lifting the blended Margin from 3.14% to the four-brand average of 3.87% would add roughly $314K on the same TO. More on how in playbook item three.

Nova88 and Goal123: same TO, very different profit, cause unconfirmed

TO is nearly identical ($6.21M vs $6.17M), but GGR differs by 47% ($451K vs $307K).

Ruling out the obvious explanation first: provider dependence is similar for both, and both lean heavily on SABA (Nova88 85.3%, Goal123 90.1%). That gap is not wide enough to explain a 47% difference in GGR.

On the same SABA book, Nova88's Margin runs 7.86%, against just 5.16% for Goal123. The final made the gap obvious: same match, Nova88 kept 21.9% and earned $57,602, while Goal123 kept only 4.8% and earned $9,348.

The data on hand only goes down to the brand level, so it does not show what actually differs about how each brand's players bet (single bets vs parlays, customer mix, VIP share). No confident claim about the cause is possible without bet-level data.

Whatever the cause turns out to be, Goal123 is the clearest Margin-improvement opportunity among the four brands. Finding and copying whatever Nova88 is doing right would grow profit without growing TO, and figuring that out should be the first data project before the next tournament.

Bwing: smallest brand, healthy fundamentals, just needs scale

TO is only 57% of Goal123's, and Margin at 4.77% sits close to Goal123's number too.

Its dependence on SABA is also the lowest of the four (79.7%), meaning its volume is not riding on a single provider.

The stronger evidence is its Margin trend across the tournament: 4.40% in the group stage, dropping to 0.77% in the Round of 32, recovering to 3.03% in the Round of 16, 8.01% in the quarterfinals, and climbing to 29.87% in the semifinal, tracking the same low-early-high-late curve as 12bet, Nova88, and Goal123.

Matching that broader pattern suggests Bwing's customer base and odds response are healthy, not just quietly stable because a few unusual bets happened to cancel out. The real difference between Bwing and the other three brands is scale, not underlying health, which means its growth path looks different from 12bet's or Goal123's: it needs volume, not a fix to how it books bets.

Provider picture: SABA dominates, 9W finished in the red

SABA takes eight to nine out of every ten dollars wagered across all four brands.

BTI, the second-largest provider, holds 12.1% of 12bet's TO but only 1.8% Margin; Pinnacle holds 5.5% at 1.64% Margin. Low-Margin providers like these concentrate almost entirely in 12bet, which is the direct reason 12bet's overall Margin lands at the bottom.

9W finished the whole tournament in the red on 12bet (Margin -6.6%), and its renewal is not in question, but the reason for the loss is worth understanding before the next tournament.

A few explanations are plausible. Small volume can produce statistical noise: 9W's volume is far below SABA's, so a handful of big winning bets could flip the total negative on their own. Odds could be priced too generously: if 9W's prices were systematically closer to true probability than they should be, the loss would be structural rather than bad luck. Or a specific promotion, or a concentration of large bettors, could be exposed on this one provider.

Confirming which one it is means pulling 9W's match-by-match profit and loss to see whether the loss concentrates in a handful of matches or spreads evenly, comparing 9W's odds against a reference price such as Pinnacle's, and checking whether any promotion is specifically tied to 9W.

2028 Euros, 2030 World Cup: earning more than this tournament

Here is a piece of good luck that costs nothing. This tournament was hosted across the US, Canada, and Mexico, putting matches at 3am to 9am GMT+8; bettors either stayed up or missed them. The 2028 Euros run in the UK and Ireland, and the 2030 World Cup is hosted across Spain, Portugal, and Morocco. European afternoon and evening kickoffs will land at 9pm to the early hours in Asia, generally an easier window to reach active bettors during (whether that is actually this platform's peak betting window would need a look at historical hour-by-hour volume to confirm). Same tournament, but the time zone alone could widen the pool of reachable active bettors. Luck like that is free, but only pays off if the platform is ready to catch it. The five items below are all built from this tournament's 104 matches.

1. Bet heavy on the knockout rounds: the schedule locks in a year out

The four brands' knockout-round average sits at 1.6 to 2.1x their group-stage average, and Margin turns positive across the board from the quarterfinals on.

A single semifinal's GGR outweighs a full day of the group stage. And the knockout rounds' dates, match count, and kickoff times are all locked in the moment the schedule is published, a rare marketing window that can be planned a full year ahead.

How this tournament's marketing budget was actually allocated, and whether it concentrated on the knockout rounds, is not something the betting data can show; only MKT's data can answer that. Step one before the next tournament is understanding MKT's actual spend pattern this time and comparing it against this betting data, to confirm whether resources landed where they mattered. If spend turns out to have been spread evenly, the next tournament should flip that: cheap content to keep daily activity alive during the group stage, with real acquisition and CRM budget locked into the two weeks after the quarterfinals, something that can go on the 2028 calendar right now.

2. Fill the schedule gaps: this tournament gave away five full days

5 of the 39 days carried no matches, 8 days had only one, and the final 13 days had just 10 matches total.

Betting volume on a gap day runs to zero, and that is exactly where players' app habits break. Before the next tournament, build an off-day content calendar: parlays on concurrent leagues, next-round prediction contests, match-recap features for days without a fixture.

The point is not to earn big on a gap day. It is to keep the habit alive so people are still around when the big matches return.

3. Lift 12bet's blended Margin: the cheapest lever available

The four brands' Margin gap runs wide, from 12bet's 3.14% up to Nova88's 7.27%.

That gap says Margin is mostly a function of how customers bet, not luck, and how customers bet can be nudged by product design.

12bet does not need to touch moneyline pricing to chase away its big spenders. The move is pushing parlays further into the betting flow: multiplying several legs' odds together compounds the built-in edge, which is why parlays carry structurally higher Margin.

On this tournament's $43M in 12bet TO, every 0.5 percentage point of blended Margin is worth about $215K in GGR, roughly 9% of the tournament's total GGR, and it is a product fix, not a marketing spend.

4. Find out why Goal123 trails Nova88 before copying anything

Same volume, same primary provider, a 47% GGR gap, and no confirmed explanation. The next move should start with finding out, not copying.

The brand comparison above was honest about this: brand-level data proves the gap exists, not why. Moving Nova88's playing structure onto Goal123 before the cause is known means betting on an unverified assumption.

The real first step before the next tournament is breaking down each brand's bet composition (single bets vs parlays, customer profile, VIP share, market mix) to find the actual source of the 47% gap. Only once that is confirmed will it be clear which part of Nova88's setup is worth copying, or whether these are simply two different customer bases that need two different playbooks.

5. Match promotional spend to Margin by stage

The group stage combined for 3.15% Margin, with 6 of 12 groups finishing negative, and the Round of 16 dropped further to 1.58%, all confirmed in this tournament's data.

These stages are already thin. If promotions happen to stack refund-style offers on top during the same window, that is a double loss. But how promotions are actually allocated, and whether they overlap with this Margin low point, is the same open question as item one: only MKT's data can answer it, and it is worth reconciling with them directly.

Looking forward, promotions during these low-Margin stages could shift toward parlay boosts or specials on goals, corners, and other derivative markets, steering volume toward higher-Margin bets. Refund-style offers could wait for the quarterfinals on, once Margin turns positive across the board; the same budget would earn back differently. Pinning down exactly which markets the low-Margin losses come from still needs bet-level breakdown (see above) before deciding what to tighten.

Four things to bring to 2028:
  1. The knockout schedule locks in a year ahead. Commit marketing budget to those weeks instead of spreading it evenly across the tournament.
  2. Have the gap-day content calendar ready before kickoff so player habits do not break.
  3. Push 12bet's blended Margin up through parlays. That is a product fix and costs no marketing budget.
  4. Find out why Goal123 trails Nova88 by 47% before deciding whether to copy Nova88's approach.
